Ultimate Momentum v11.46 — Official User GuideWelcome to the Ultimate Momentum trading engine. This system is designed to automate portfolio construction by identifying upward-trending stocks (momentum) while strictly protecting your capital from severe market crashes using advanced risk mathematics (CVaR).This guide will walk you step-by-step through setting up the engine, optimizing its parameters, and executing your daily trading routine.Glossary of Key Terms (No Jargon Left Behind)Before we begin, here are three concepts the engine uses frequently:CVaR (Conditional Value at Risk): A mathematical measure of extreme risk. It estimates the expected loss on your absolute worst days. The engine strictly limits this to protect your capital.ADV (Average Daily Volume): A measure of liquidity (how easy it is to buy/sell a stock without moving its price). The engine filters out illiquid stocks to prevent you from getting trapped in a trade.Regime Score: A macro-economic indicator (from 0 to 1). A high score means the overall stock market is healthy (Bull market), prompting the engine to invest your cash. A low score means the market is crashing or highly volatile (Bear market), prompting the engine to move your money to cash.Phase 1: Installation & SetupStep 1: Install PythonEnsure you have Python installed on your computer. Production Notes (v11.46+)

1) Slippage parameter meaning
- `ROUND_TRIP_SLIPPAGE_BPS` is round-trip cost (buy + sell) in basis points.
- One-way cost used during execution is half of this value.

2) Custom Screener reliability
- Screener.in can intermittently fail due to anti-bot protections (HTTP 403 / Cloudflare).
- For production, prefer local `custom_screener.csv` (or `custom_screener.txt`) as the primary source.
- Treat direct Screener URL scraping as a convenience helper only.

3) Historical universe parquet requirement
- Backtests avoid survivorship bias only when historical universe parquet files are present.
- Required files:
  - `data/historical_nifty500.parquet`
  - `data/historical_nse_total.parquet`
- Required schema:
  - Index: `DatetimeIndex` with snapshot date (point-in-time effective date).
  - Column: `tickers` containing a list-like collection of NSE symbols for that date.
- Example row meaning: at index date `2021-06-30`, `tickers` must contain exactly the symbols tradable in that universe on/just before that date.

4) Reproducible environments
- Install from `requirements-lock.txt` for reproducible dependency versions:
  - `pip install -r requirements-lock.txt`

5) Paper mode and bootstrap helper
- For safe dry-runs, start the dashboard with `python daily_workflow.py --paper` to disable portfolio state file writes, and if you are setting up backtests for the first time run `python historical_builder.py` to bootstrap both required historical universe files (`data/historical_nifty500.parquet` and `data/historical_nse_total.parquet`).
